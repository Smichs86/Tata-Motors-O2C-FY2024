# Project Summary
## Tata Motors - Order-to-Cash Performance Analysis | FY2024

---

## Executive Summary

This project analyzes Tata Motors Limited's Order-to-Cash (O2C) 
performance using FY2024 publicly available audited financial data.
The analysis reveals that Tata Motors operates at world-class O2C 
efficiency - 31 days faster than the industry average - generating 
a ₹37,039 Crore working capital competitive advantage.

---

## Business Problem

How does Tata Motors' O2C performance compare to industry benchmarks,
and what structural factors drive their competitive advantage?

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1 - World-Class DSO Performance
- Tata Motors DSO: **14.1 days**
- Industry Average: **45 days**
- Competitive Advantage: **31 days faster**
- This is not incremental improvement - it is structural superiority

### Finding 2 - Massive Working Capital Advantage
- Working Capital Freed: **₹37,039 Crores**
- Daily Revenue: ₹1,199.8 Cr/day
- This cash is deployed for EV R&D and debt reduction
- Enables growth without new borrowing

### Finding 3 - JLR is the Primary Driver
- JLR Revenue Mix: **68.21%** of total revenue
- JLR customers (premium automotive) pay significantly faster
- This business model advantage cannot be easily replicated
- Risk threshold: JLR mix must stay above 60%

### Finding 4 - Continuous Improvement Culture
- FY2022 DSO: 20.2 days
- FY2023 DSO: 16.6 days (-17.8% YoY)
- FY2024 DSO: 14.1 days (-15.1% YoY)
- 3-Year Improvement: **30% reduction**
- Demonstrates operational excellence, not one-time improvement

### Finding 5 - Exceptional Credit Quality
- Bad Debt Ratio: **0.53%**
- Industry acceptable range: 1-2%
- Receivables Turnover: **25.83x** (world-class)
